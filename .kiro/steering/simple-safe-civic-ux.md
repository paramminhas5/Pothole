# Simple, Safe Civic UX

## Product standard

- Design every public journey to be understandable without civic, legal, or technical knowledge.
- Use plain words, one clear primary action per screen, short sentences, large touch targets, visible progress, and forgiving error recovery.
- Use a child-comprehensible usability benchmark: a 5-year-old should understand the visual hierarchy and an 8-year-old should understand basic navigation and instructions during supervised testing.
- Do not use childish styling or language; the product must remain dignified, trustworthy, and useful to adults.
- Default to mobile-first, low-bandwidth, low-end-device, keyboard, and screen-reader usability.
- Explain what happens next, who can see submitted information, and how to cancel or get help before consequential actions.

## Child safeguarding

- Child-comprehensible design does not mean high-risk civic, protest, legal, shelter, contact, group, or location workflows are open to unsupervised children.
- High-risk participation requires an explicit age/safeguarding policy, data-minimizing age assurance where appropriate, restricted discovery/contact/location, trained moderation, and qualified legal review.
- Never publicly expose a child's identity, school, contact details, precise location, group membership, attendance, or political activity.
- Do not use behavioral advertising, engagement manipulation, streaks, public leaderboards, or persuasive dark patterns, especially for minors.
- Provide a clear safe-exit path and age-appropriate help guidance.

## Definition of done

- Core flows work end to end with keyboard and screen reader.
- Core flows are tested on narrow screens and low-end mobile/network profiles.
- Labels and errors use plain language and do not rely on icons, color, or legal jargon alone.
- Usability testing includes adults with low digital literacy; child testing, if conducted, is supervised, consented, privacy-preserving, and limited to low-risk navigation/content comprehension.
